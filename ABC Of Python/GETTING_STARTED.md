# Getting Started with Emperor's Python World

Welcome! This guide will help you navigate the curriculum and use the resources effectively, whether you're on a phone or a computer. No prior experience required — just dedication.

---

## 1. Explore the Python Syllabus
Open the `Python_Curriculum.md` file to see the full course outline.  
Each module and topic is listed so you always know where you are and what’s coming next.

---

## 2. Activate Your AI Mentor
The file `Python_Curriculum_[Ai_Prompt].md` contains a complete prompt designed to turn any modern AI assistant into your personal Python mentor.  
- Copy the entire content of that file.  
- Paste it as the first message in a new conversation with your AI of choice.  
- The AI will then guide you through the syllabus using the "two bricks at a time" method, just as it did for the course creator.

No configuration needed — it's plug‑and‑play.

---

## 3. Use YouTube as a Visual Partner
After reading a concept or completing a practice task, search for short Python tutorials on YouTube. Watching someone code the same idea helps reinforce what you’ve typed with your own hands.

---

## 4. Strengthen Your Foundation with Books
The following books are highly recommended companions. They explain concepts in depth and offer plenty of exercises.

- **Python Crash Course** by Eric Matthes  
  A hands-on, project-based introduction.
- **Automate the Boring Stuff with Python** by Al Sweigart  
  Practical automation tasks that make Python immediately useful.
- **Think Python** by Allen B. Downey  
  A clear, methodical approach to computational thinking.

All three are available in physical, e‑book, and free online formats.
